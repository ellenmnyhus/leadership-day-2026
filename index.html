Breakout Option A
AI Resilience Scoring
Participants score their own company against Verdane's proprietary AI Resilience Framework and identify their single biggest vulnerability. Practical, structured, and grounded in real portfolio data.
The Framework
Tier 1 — Structural Moats (Yes/No)
If any YES → company scores 7–8 / 8
T1.1 — Is value delivered through integrated and proprietary hardware AND software? T1.2 — Does the company benefit from strong network effects? T1.3 — Does the company deliver differentiated physical product value that AI cannot bypass with software alone?
The Framework
Tier 2 — Supporting Moats (scored 1–8)
Two or more at 7+ indicates strong resilience
T2.1 — Does the product create an audit trail regulators and auditors rely on? T2.2 — Does the product encode expertise that takes years to build? T2.3 — Is the product the system of record? T2.4 — Would operations stop without this product? T2.5 — Does the company have a strong brand and a direct contractual relationship with customers? Score interpretation: • 7–8 / 8 — Structurally resilient. AI is more opportunity than threat. • 5–6 / 8 — Moderate. Some protection but active strategy needed now. • 1–4 / 8 — Vulnerable. Immediate action required on defence and offence.
Session Structure · 75 min
How the Session Runs
CEO track: Ellen + Caroline · COB track: Niklas + Sascha
Introduction (10 min) — Hand out scorecards, explain two tiers. Key framing: "A low score isn't a verdict — it's a starting point." Individual self-scoring (10 min) — Each participant scores their company silently across all 8 dimensions. Small group discussion (40 min) — Groups of 4–5, pre-mixed across sectors: 1. Share your score and the dimension you found hardest to answer — why? 2. Where in your business is AI most likely to create value in the next 12 months? 3. What is the one structural thing you could do to improve your resilience score in 2 years? Share-back (10 min) — 2–3 groups share key insights. Individual call to action (5 min) — Each writes: "The one thing I will do in my company as a result of today."
COB Track Note
For COBs, redirect discussion question 3 to: "What governance or board-level decision does your score suggest you should be having?" Less about operational action, more about asking the CEO the right questions.
Breakout Option C
The Human Edge
What will still be human at the top? Erik Salvador opens with a short keynote, then three sharp provocations drive honest group discussion.
Session Format · 75 min
How This Session Runs
Erik Salvador · CEO & COB tracks together
Erik opens with a 10–15 minute keynote drawing on McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte and Harvard research to frame the central question: when AI democratises analytical capacity, what makes a leader irreplaceable? Three sharp provocations are then discussed in groups of 5–6. Each has a research stat, a core question, and discussion prompts. No slides for the discussion — just the provocation cards and honest conversation. Closes with a room-wide share-back and individual written commitments: "The one thing I will do differently as a leader in the next 30 days."
Intended Purpose
Make the disruption debate personal — not "what should our company do" but "what does this mean for me, as a leader, right now?" Works especially well after the hackathon: participants have just built something with AI, and now the question shifts to what that means for how they lead.
01
Your leadership advantage just became available to everyone.
92% plan to increase AI investment. Only 1% consider themselves truly ready. — McKinsey
AI democratises analytical capacity. The data, market insight, and strategic analysis that used to make the best leaders stand out — AI now gives that to everyone. CEOs and CTOs already drive 44.5% of all AI decisions. The bottleneck is not technology. It is leadership.
"What does your leadership team offer that AI cannot replicate — and are you all agreed on the answer?"
What is the one thing you personally bring that AI genuinely cannot substitute?
Is your leadership team aligned on this — or does everyone have a different answer?
What does your answer mean for how you spend your time?
02
Your organisation is using AI. Your frontline isn't.
75% of senior leaders use AI weekly. Only 51% of frontline employees do. — BCG "Silicon Ceiling"
When employees lack approved tools, 54% find alternatives on their own — creating security risk. Positive AI attitudes jump from 15% to 55% when leaders actively endorse it. Frontline managers are 3× more worried about AI than senior leaders. What looks like resistance is usually a leadership gap.
"Are you personally leading AI transformation — or delegating it and hoping it happens?"
What is the gap between how you use AI and how your team uses it?
What would it take for your frontline managers to feel excited rather than threatened?
What have you personally stopped doing because AI does it better — and have you told your team?
03
Would you let an AI represent you in a board meeting?
Only 1 in 5 companies has mature governance for autonomous AI agents. — Deloitte 2026
Digital "agentic twins" — autonomous AI acting on your behalf — are here. Nearly half of Fortune 100 companies now mention AI risk in board oversight reports — a 3× increase in one year. The question is not whether AI will act on your behalf. It already does. The question is how far you are willing to let it go.
"Where is your personal boundary — and does your board know where the company's boundary is?"
Where in your organisation are AI agents already making decisions — do you know where all of them are?
What is on your board agenda about AI governance — and is it enough?
If your AI made a decision that harmed a customer or employee, who is responsible?
Closing Question · Harvard Business School
Change Fitness
HBS defines "change fitness" as the capacity to absorb significant and sustained change — the meta-skill that determines whether leaders and organisations succeed or stagnate. Minimum bar: every leader needs at least a 30% digital and AI mindset. Warning: when AI takes over tasks that gave people meaning, work risks becoming less meaningful. Leaders must shift from optimising processes to designing them.
Closing Question for the Room
"On a scale of 1–10 — honestly — how change-fit are you as a leader right now? Not your company. You. And what does your organisation need from you in the next 12 months that only a human can provide?"

Initiative 5
"You cannot lead a transformation you don't understand from the inside."
Personal AI Coaching — "Your AI PT"
Owner: Ellen Nyhus + Caroline Ohlsson + Talent team · Survey sent week after Leadership Day
Inspired by the CEO of Swedish grocery chain ICA, who recently set up a personal AI coach with biweekly check-ins to upskill on how to use AI in his own work. Research is clear: positive AI attitudes jump from 15% to 55% when leaders personally engage and model it. Following Leadership Day, Verdane surveys CEOs and COBs to identify who wants personal AI coaching. Elevate matches them with an AI coach — from within the Elevate/partner network, or via top Nordic and DACH university AI programmes (students get real-world exposure, leaders get hands-on support). Biweekly sessions, 45–60 min, structured around the leader's own priorities and company context. 3–6 month programme per participant. Coaching also covers the workforce transformation dimension — structured support on how to lead AI-driven workforce change, how to plan for headcount and role redesign proactively, how to bring HR into the process before it becomes a crisis, and how to communicate with the organisation honestly and with care.
